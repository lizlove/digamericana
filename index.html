About us

Founded in 2013, Americana Music Productions leverages relationships with the world's top cultural institutions, record companies, lawyers, scholars, and artists to preserve, present, and celebrate America's musical heritage. We approach each project with respect for the artist, the creative act, and the community from which it emanates. From A Love Supreme to "The Sex Machine," our knowledge has no bounds.

Our Work

Releasing a limited edition LP of Mable Hillery and Johnny Shines at the 1975 Miami Blues Festival
Presenting on The 1949 VD Radio Project and catching the eye of cultural critic Greil Marcus
Licensing photos and film footage for the National Blues Museum in St. Louis
Introducing the 1969 Ann Arbor Blues Festival to attendees of the Barnard-Columbia Blues Symposium
Publishing a piece on the "Southern Journey" in Sounding Out! to celebrate Alan Lomax's centennial
Producing radio programs on Willie Dixon, Magic Sam, and Reverend Robert Wilkins for WKCR-FM in NYC
Researching the work of feminist music historian Rosetta Reitz and her flagship Rosetta Records
Interviewing blues great Dennis Binder about music and integration in Lawton, OK and the Southwest
Interviewing the late New Orleans drummer Smokey Johnson about his life, Fats Domino, and early Motown
