About us

Americana Music Productions (AMP) was born of the reality that as rich as the known landscape of American music is, there are far more riches that remain unknown, unsung, and undiscovered. AMP approaches each project with respect for the creative act, the artist, and the community from which it emanates. Founded in 2013, AMP leverages relationships with the world's top archives, record companies, museums, filmmakers, scholars, photographers, lawyers, writers, and musicians to address complex problems (like preservation, licensing, and resources), enabling access in the service of preserving America's musical heritage.

Some of our work includes:
A limited edition LP of Mable Hillery and Johnny Shines at the 1975 Miami Blues Festival
Licensing photos and film footage for the National Blues Museum in St. Louis
Researching the work of feminist music historian Rosetta Reitz and her flagship Rosetta Records
Interviewing blues and R&B great Dennis Binder about music and integration in Lawton, OK and the Southwest
Introducing the 1969 Ann Arbor Blues Festival to attendees of the Barnard-Columbia Blues Symposium
Publishing a piece on the "Southern Journey" in Sounding Out! to celebrate Alan Lomax's centennial
Interviewing the late New Orleans drummer Smokey Johnson about his life, Fats Domino, and early Motown
Producing a 6-hour Willie Dixon Centennial radio program on WKCR-FM in NYC
